About ThinkLab

Where Evidence Meets the Real World

ThinkLab: Economics & Policy is an independent research organization dedicated to producing high-quality economic research that matters — not just in academic journals, but in real policy conversations and public understanding.

We believe the gap between academic research and public discourse is one of the most urgent problems in modern policy. ThinkLab exists to close that gap.

Whether you're a researcher seeking collaboration, a student looking to gain research experience, or a policymaker searching for evidence-based guidance — ThinkLab is built for you.

Research Areas

What We Study

Our research spans multiple domains at the intersection of economics, environment, and public policy — with a focus on real-world implications.

01
🌡️

Climate Economics

Studying the economic consequences of climate shocks, environmental policy, and the links between climate change and industrial structure in developing economies.

Climate Shocks Industrial Policy Developing Countries
02
📊

Development Economics

Examining economic growth, poverty, structural transformation, and the role of institutions and policy in driving sustainable development outcomes.

Growth Institutions Structural Change

Translating rigorous economic research into actionable policy recommendations across fiscal, trade, labor, and environmental policy domains.

Working Paper 2024

Climate Shocks, Input-Output Linkages, and Manufacturing Contraction: Evidence from Developing Countries

Muhammad Taky Tahmid

This paper examines how climate shocks propagate through input-output linkages to affect manufacturing sectors in developing countries, providing empirical evidence on the economic consequences of climate-induced disruptions.
